Code of Safe Practice

The IANA Code of Safe Practice establishes essential safety standards for Neuro Acupuncture practice. All IANA members must adhere to these guidelines to ensure patient safety and maintain professional standards.

1. Clinical Assessment & Contraindications

  • Conduct thorough neurological assessment before treatment
  • Obtain comprehensive medical history including current medications and diagnoses
  • Identify and respect absolute and relative contraindications to Neuro Acupuncture
  • Assess patient capacity to consent to treatment
  • Recognize situations requiring medical referral before treatment
  • Document all assessment findings in clinical records

2. Infection Control & Hygiene

  • Maintain appropriate hand hygiene protocols before and after patient contact
  • Use only sterile, single-use acupuncture needles
  • Follow proper needle disposal procedures using sharps containers
  • Maintain clean treatment environment and equipment
  • Implement appropriate infection control measures for clinical practice
  • Comply with local health and safety regulations

3. Needling Safety

  • Demonstrate competence in clean needle technique
  • Know and respect safe needling depths for scalp acupuncture
  • Avoid dangerous zones including fontanelles in appropriate patients
  • Use appropriate needle gauge and length for clinical indication
  • Maintain appropriate needle angle for scalp acupuncture techniques
  • Monitor patient response during needle insertion and retention
  • Remove needles carefully and check all needles are accounted for

4. Patient Monitoring & Response

  • Maintain visual observation of patients during treatment
  • Recognize and appropriately manage adverse reactions
  • Know how to respond to vasovagal reactions and fainting
  • Maintain emergency contact information and procedures
  • Document any adverse events or unusual responses
  • Provide appropriate aftercare advice following treatment

5. Clinical Competence

  • Practice only techniques for which adequate training has been received
  • Maintain current knowledge of neurological conditions treated
  • Understand neurological assessment and red flags
  • Recognize situations requiring immediate medical referral
  • Maintain competence through regular continuing professional development
  • Seek supervision or consultation when facing unfamiliar clinical situations

6. Informed Consent

  • Obtain valid informed consent before treatment
  • Explain treatment procedures, potential benefits, and risks
  • Ensure patient understands and can ask questions
  • Respect patient right to decline or withdraw consent
  • Document consent in clinical records
  • Obtain guardian consent when treating minors or incapacitated adults

7. Record Keeping

  • Maintain accurate, legible, contemporaneous clinical records
  • Document assessment findings, treatment provided, and patient response
  • Record informed consent and any contraindications identified
  • Maintain confidentiality of patient records
  • Comply with data protection regulations
  • Retain records for appropriate period as per local regulations

8. Professional Boundaries

  • Maintain appropriate professional boundaries with patients
  • Ensure appropriate chaperoning when treating sensitive areas
  • Provide treatment in appropriate clinical environment
  • Respect patient privacy and dignity
  • Recognize and appropriately manage transference issues
  • Never engage in sexual or inappropriate relationships with patients

9. Equipment & Environment

  • Maintain appropriate clinical environment and equipment
  • Ensure treatment couches and equipment are safe and well-maintained
  • Provide appropriate emergency equipment and supplies
  • Maintain appropriate clinical waste disposal procedures
  • Ensure adequate lighting and ventilation in treatment rooms
  • Comply with health and safety regulations

10. Professional Insurance & Governance

  • Maintain appropriate professional indemnity insurance
  • Comply with local registration and regulatory requirements
  • Participate in clinical governance and quality improvement activities
  • Report significant adverse events to appropriate authorities
  • Maintain professional development portfolio
  • Engage in regular reflective practice and supervision

Safety Commitment

Patient safety is the highest priority for all IANA members. Adherence to this Code of Safe Practice is mandatory for all members and is subject to review as part of membership requirements.

Members who have concerns about safety in practice should contact IANA for guidance and support.

This Code of Safe Practice should be read in conjunction with the IANA Code of Ethics.
