What is Neuro Acupuncture?

Neuro acupuncture represents a specialised approach to acupuncture that focuses on modulating brain and nervous system function to address neurological conditions, chronic pain, and functional limitations. Rather than treating local symptoms in isolation, neuro acupuncture uses precise, evidence-informed techniques—particularly scalp acupuncture—to influence central nervous system activity and promote functional recovery.

The field of neuro acupuncture has evolved significantly over recent decades, drawing on both traditional acupuncture principles and contemporary neuroscience. However, the lack of unified professional standards, inconsistent training quality, and limited awareness of neuro acupuncture's clinical potential have historically hindered its recognition and integration into mainstream healthcare. The International Association for Neuro Acupuncture (IANA) exists to address these gaps: establishing internationally recognised professional standards, accrediting high-quality training programmes, and advancing evidence-based practice to ensure that patients receive safe, effective, and consistent care from qualified practitioners.

Conditions Treated with Neuro Acupuncture

Neuro acupuncture encompasses specialised techniques for treating a wide range of neurological conditions and symptoms. By modulating nervous system function, neuro acupuncture can help reduce symptoms, improve functional recovery, and enhance quality of life across diverse presentations:

Stroke Recovery

Following a stroke, patients often experience motor weakness, speech difficulties, and functional limitations. Neuro acupuncture techniques can help facilitate motor recovery, improve coordination, and support functional restoration through targeted nervous system stimulation.

Movement Disorders

Conditions such as Parkinson's disease result in movement difficulties, tremor, and loss of motor control. Neuro acupuncture can help reduce tremor, improve movement initiation, and enhance overall motor function and stability.

Chronic Pain

Chronic pain—including musculoskeletal pain and neuropathic conditions—often involves altered pain processing in the brain. Neuro acupuncture modulates pain signalling pathways, offering lasting relief by addressing the neurological basis of pain rather than local symptoms alone.

Migraine & Headaches

Migraine and chronic headaches involve dysregulation of neural pathways controlling pain and inflammation. Neuro acupuncture can reduce migraine frequency and intensity by rebalancing nervous system function and reducing neural hyperexcitability.

Multiple Sclerosis

Multiple sclerosis affects diverse nervous system functions, causing fatigue, spasticity, sensory disturbances, and mobility limitations. Neuro acupuncture can help manage these symptoms and support functional independence through nervous system modulation.

Neurological Rehabilitation

Traumatic brain injury, peripheral neuropathy, balance disorders, and cognitive difficulties benefit from neuro acupuncture's capacity to promote neural plasticity, restore function, and support recovery across multiple neurological systems.

Professional Development

IANA members benefit from ongoing access to continuing professional development, advanced training workshops, specialist seminars, and collaborative learning opportunities designed to enhance clinical skills, deepen evidence-based knowledge, and maintain professional currency in neuro acupuncture practice.

Training Accreditation

IANA accredits neuro acupuncture training programmes that meet our rigorous professional standards. Accreditation ensures that practitioners worldwide receive consistent, high-quality education in evidence-based techniques, safety protocols, and clinical best practice.

Standards Setting

IANA establishes internationally recognised standards for professional conduct, clinical practice, and competence. Through our Code of Ethics and Code of Safe Practice, we ensure that all IANA members uphold the highest standards of patient safety, professional integrity, and clinical excellence.

Research & Evidence Base

IANA is committed to advancing the evidence base for neuro acupuncture. We support access to current research, facilitate clinical studies, disseminate evidence-based protocols, and promote the integration of neuro acupuncture into mainstream healthcare through rigorous, peer-reviewed evidence.

Recognised Neuro Acupuncture Modalities

IANA recognises practitioners trained in established Neuro Acupuncture approaches, including scalp acupuncture systems and contemporary neurological acupuncture protocols:

NeuroAlign Methods

Contemporary evidence-based protocols specifically developed to address neurological conditions and chronic pain through targeted nervous system modulation:

NeuroRelief Method

A comprehensive, brain-centred approach to chronic pain management that recognises pain as a neurological output. NeuroRelief combines scalp acupuncture targeting specific pain-processing brain regions with distal acupuncture techniques to modulate pain signalling, reduce central sensitisation, and provide lasting relief for patients with persistent pain conditions.

NeuroRehab Method

A structured rehabilitation protocol designed to support functional recovery following neurological injury or disease. NeuroRehab employs scalp acupuncture and movement-based techniques to promote neural plasticity, restore motor control, and enhance functional independence in conditions such as stroke, Parkinson's disease, and traumatic brain injury.

NeuroBalance Method

An auricular (ear) acupuncture-based approach to addressing mental health conditions, emotional dysregulation, and addiction. NeuroBalance works by regulating stress response systems and promoting emotional resilience through precise stimulation of acupuncture points linked to emotional and cognitive processing centres.

Scalp Acupuncture Systems

Zhu's Scalp Acupuncture

Comprehensive scalp microsystem developed by Dr Zhu Ming-Qing, utilising specific scalp zones corresponding to neurological functions.

Fang's Scalp Acupuncture

Advanced scalp acupuncture technique by Dr Fang Yun-Peng, focusing on precise scalp microsystem mapping.

Tang's Scalp Acupuncture

Specialised scalp acupuncture approach by Dr Tang Song-Yan, emphasising specific stimulation techniques.

YNSA

Yamamoto New Scalp Acupuncture, a refined microsystem approach developed by Dr Toshikatsu Yamamoto.
