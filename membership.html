Professional Membership

IANA membership provides professional recognition for qualified practitioners of neuro-acupuncture modalities. Members benefit from continuing professional development opportunities, access to current research, and inclusion in our international practitioner directory.

Membership Benefits

Professional Recognition

IANA membership certificate and listing in the international practitioner directory with professional credentials verification.

Continuing Professional Development

Access to CPD programmes, specialist workshops, webinars, and advanced training opportunities in neuro-acupuncture practice.

Research & Evidence

Current research publications, clinical studies, evidence-based protocols, and case reports in neuro-acupuncture.

Professional Network

Connection with neuro-acupuncture practitioners globally through member forums, conferences, and collaborative initiatives.

Professional Resources

Clinical guidance documents, professional standards framework, and ethical practice guidelines.

Member Recognition

IANA member designation for professional use, including digital badge and certificate of membership.

Membership Requirements

Professional membership requires demonstration of appropriate qualifications and specialist training:

Educational Requirements

  • Hold current registration with a recognised professional acupuncture body
  • Possess valid acupuncture qualification from an accredited institution
  • Maintain professional indemnity insurance as required by jurisdiction

Specialist Training

Documented training in at least one recognised neuro-acupuncture modality:

  • Zhu's Scalp Acupuncture
  • Fang's Scalp Acupuncture
  • Tang's Scalp Acupuncture
  • YNSA (Yamamoto New Scalp Acupuncture)
  • NeuroAlign Methods (NeuroRelief, NeuroRehab, or NeuroBalance)

Clinical Experience

  • Minimum 100 hours documented clinical practice in neuro-acupuncture techniques
  • Demonstrated competence in neurological assessment and treatment planning
  • Understanding of neurological conditions and rehabilitation principles

Professional Standing

  • Good standing with professional registration body
  • Current professional indemnity insurance
  • Commitment to IANA code of ethics and professional conduct
  • Agreement to maintain continuing professional development

Student Member Requirements

  • Current enrolment in accredited acupuncture or Traditional Chinese Medicine programme
  • Proof of student status (student identification or institutional verification)
  • Intent to pursue neuro-acupuncture specialisation

Application Process

How to Apply

  1. Review membership requirements and select appropriate membership level
  2. Prepare required documentation (qualifications, training certificates, professional registration)
  3. Complete the application form below
  4. Submit supporting documentation
  5. Pay applicable membership fee
  6. Await review by IANA membership committee (typically 5-7 working days)
  7. Receive membership confirmation and certificate upon approval
