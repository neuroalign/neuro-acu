Code of Ethics

The IANA Code of Ethics establishes the fundamental ethical principles that guide professional conduct for all IANA members. Members are required to uphold these standards in all aspects of their professional practice.

1. Patient-Centred Care

  • Place patient wellbeing and safety as the highest priority in all clinical decisions
  • Respect patient autonomy, dignity, and right to informed consent
  • Maintain patient confidentiality in accordance with applicable data protection regulations
  • Provide care without discrimination based on age, gender, ethnicity, religion, disability, or socioeconomic status
  • Recognise and work within the limits of personal competence

2. Professional Competence

  • Maintain current knowledge and skills through continuing professional development
  • Practice only within areas of demonstrated competence and appropriate training
  • Refer patients to other healthcare professionals when appropriate
  • Maintain accurate and contemporaneous clinical records
  • Base clinical practice on current evidence and established protocols

3. Professional Integrity

  • Act with honesty and integrity in all professional interactions
  • Provide accurate information about qualifications, training, and scope of practice
  • Avoid making unrealistic claims about treatment outcomes
  • Maintain appropriate professional boundaries with patients
  • Declare any conflicts of interest that may affect professional judgment

4. Professional Conduct

  • Maintain professional indemnity insurance appropriate to scope of practice
  • Comply with all relevant laws, regulations, and professional standards
  • Respect the professional standing and reputation of colleagues
  • Engage in ethical advertising and marketing practices
  • Report concerns about professional conduct that may affect patient safety

5. Research & Evidence-Based Practice

  • Base clinical practice on current research evidence and established protocols
  • Contribute to the evidence base through ethical research and clinical documentation
  • Obtain appropriate ethical approval for research involving human participants
  • Present research findings accurately and honestly
  • Acknowledge the work and contributions of others appropriately

6. Professional Development

  • Engage in regular continuing professional development activities
  • Maintain competence in Neuro Acupuncture techniques and neurological assessment
  • Stay current with developments in neurology and neurological rehabilitation
  • Participate in professional supervision and peer review when appropriate
  • Support the professional development of colleagues and students

7. Collaboration & Communication

  • Communicate effectively with patients, families, and healthcare colleagues
  • Work collaboratively within multidisciplinary healthcare teams
  • Provide appropriate information to referring healthcare professionals
  • Respect the expertise and contributions of other healthcare disciplines
  • Maintain professional communication standards in all interactions

Ethical Conduct Requirements

All IANA members agree to abide by this Code of Ethics as a condition of membership. Violations of ethical standards may result in membership review and potential sanctions, including membership suspension or termination.

Members are encouraged to seek guidance from IANA when facing ethical dilemmas in practice.

This Code of Ethics should be read in conjunction with the IANA Code of Safe Practice.
