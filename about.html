About the International Association for Neuro Acupuncture

Where ancient wisdom meets modern neuroscience. IANA is the global professional body championing excellence in neurological acupuncture practice, uniting practitioners who are transforming lives through brain-centred healing.

Our Story

Born from a vision to elevate neuro acupuncture from niche specialty to mainstream neurological intervention, IANA represents a new era in complementary healthcare. We're not just another professional association—we're a global movement of pioneering practitioners who see the nervous system not as a mystery to manage, but as a pathway to profound healing.

Every day, our members witness transformations that conventional approaches said were impossible: stroke survivors regaining movement, chronic pain patients finding relief after years of suffering, and neurological conditions responding to treatment in ways that challenge traditional understanding.

The Techniques That Define Our Field

IANA recognizes practitioners trained in evidence-informed neuro acupuncture approaches that have demonstrated clinical effectiveness. Each modality represents decades of development by pioneering clinicians.

NeuroAlign Methods

Contemporary evidence-based protocols developed specifically for neurological applications in today's clinical landscape:

NeuroRelief Method

A brain-centred scalp acupuncture approach targeting pain-processing regions to interrupt chronic pain cycles and restore natural pain regulation.

NeuroRehab Method

Structured rehabilitation protocol combining precise scalp acupuncture with movement re-education to support functional recovery following neurological injury or disease.

NeuroBalance Method

Auricular (ear) acupuncture approach addressing mental health conditions, emotional dysregulation, and addiction through nervous system rebalancing.

Classical Scalp Acupuncture Systems

Zhu's Scalp Acupuncture

Developed by Dr Zhu Ming-Qing, this comprehensive approach to scalp acupuncture utilizes specific scalp zones corresponding to neurological functions. Dr Zhu's meticulous mapping of scalp regions revolutionized the understanding of how scalp stimulation can influence neurological recovery and function.

Fang's Scalp Acupuncture

Created by Dr Fang Yun-Peng, this refined approach emphasizes precise anatomical mapping and specific stimulation techniques tailored to neurological conditions. Dr Fang's clinical innovations have made scalp acupuncture more accessible and effective for diverse patient populations.

Tang's Scalp Acupuncture

Pioneered by Dr Tang Song-Yan, this system focuses on specialized needle manipulation techniques and specific scalp zone activation. Dr Tang's contributions expanded the therapeutic possibilities of scalp acupuncture in clinical practice.

YNSA

Developed by Dr Toshikatsu Yamamoto, Yamamoto New Scalp Acupuncture represents a distinctive microsystem approach with unique diagnostic and treatment protocols. Dr Yamamoto's systematic methodology has gained worldwide recognition for its clinical effectiveness.
