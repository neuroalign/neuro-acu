About the International Association for Neuro Acupuncture

The International Association for Neuro Acupuncture (IANA) is the professional body for practitioners specialising in neurological acupuncture modalities. Based in the United Kingdom, IANA provides professional recognition, continuing education, and research support to qualified practitioners worldwide.

Our Mission

IANA is dedicated to the advancement of Neuro Acupuncture worldwide. We maintain professional standards in Neuro Acupuncture practice through:

  • Establishing and upholding professional standards for Neuro Acupuncture practitioners
  • Providing accreditation of Neuro Acupuncture courses that meet IANA requirements
  • Facilitating continuing professional development and specialist training opportunities
  • Promoting evidence-based practice through research collaboration and clinical studies
  • Supporting knowledge exchange among Neuro Acupuncture specialists globally
  • Advancing public understanding of Neuro Acupuncture as a clinical intervention
  • Ensuring ethical practice and professional accountability

Course Accreditation

IANA provides accreditation for Neuro Acupuncture training courses that meet our professional standards. Accreditation ensures that training programmes provide comprehensive, evidence-based education preparing practitioners for safe and effective clinical practice.

Training providers who wish to have their courses accredited can find full information on our Training Accreditation page.

Neuro Acupuncture: Clinical Applications

Neuro Acupuncture encompasses specialised acupuncture techniques targeting neurological conditions and functional optimisation. These approaches integrate traditional acupuncture principles with contemporary understanding of neurology and neurophysiology.

Clinical applications include:

  • Post-stroke rehabilitation and recovery
  • Traumatic brain injury rehabilitation
  • Movement disorders including Parkinson's disease
  • Multiple sclerosis symptom management
  • Peripheral neuropathy
  • Chronic neurological pain conditions
  • Migraine and chronic headaches
  • Balance and coordination disorders
  • Cognitive function support
  • Neurological rehabilitation following injury or illness

Recognised Modalities

IANA recognises practitioners qualified in the following Neuro Acupuncture modalities:

NeuroAlign Methods

Contemporary evidence-based protocols developed for specific neurological applications:

NeuroRelief Method

Targeted intervention protocols for neurological pain management, symptom relief, and functional improvement in pain-related neurological conditions.

NeuroRehab Method

Structured rehabilitation protocols designed for neurological recovery, functional restoration, and rehabilitation following neurological injury or illness.

NeuroBalance Method

Integrative approaches focusing on neurological homeostasis, functional optimisation, and maintenance of neurological health.

Zhu's Scalp Acupuncture

Comprehensive scalp acupuncture system developed by Dr Zhu Ming-Qing (Zhu Ming Qing). This approach utilises specific scalp zones corresponding to neurological functions and pathways, with established protocols for stroke rehabilitation and neurological conditions.

Fang's Scalp Acupuncture

Advanced scalp acupuncture technique developed by Dr Fang Yun-Peng (Fang Yunpeng), emphasising precise scalp microsystem mapping and neurological correspondence. This system includes detailed protocols for various neurological presentations.

Tang's Scalp Acupuncture

Specialised approach to scalp acupuncture by Dr Tang Song-Yan (Tang Songyan), characterised by specific stimulation techniques and point selection strategies for optimal neurological outcomes.

YNSA

Yamamoto New Scalp Acupuncture, developed by Dr Toshikatsu Yamamoto. A refined microsystem approach with specific diagnostic and treatment protocols based on scalp point palpation and neurological assessment.

Professional Standards

Clinical Excellence

Commitment to evidence-based practice, clinical competence, and continuous professional development in Neuro Acupuncture.

Professional Integrity

Adherence to ethical principles, professional accountability, and transparent clinical practice in all aspects of patient care.

Research & Innovation

Support for clinical research, evidence generation, and advancement of Neuro Acupuncture knowledge and practice.

Collaborative Practice

Facilitation of knowledge sharing, peer support, and collaborative learning among Neuro Acupuncture practitioners.

Professional Membership

IANA membership is open to qualified acupuncture practitioners with specialist training in recognised Neuro Acupuncture modalities. All members must maintain professional indemnity insurance and actively practice Neuro Acupuncture.
